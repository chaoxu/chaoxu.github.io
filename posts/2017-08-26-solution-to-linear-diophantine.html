Small L1​ norm solution to a linear Diophantine equation


Let a=(a1​,…,an​) be n integers with gcd(a1​,…,an​)=1. There exists a integral vector x=(x1​,…,xn​), such that ∑i=1n​xi​ai​=1. How large is the solution x? By Bézout’s lemma, when n=2, we can obtain that ∥x∥≤∥a∥. Here ∥⋅∥ is the L1​ norm.

However, I could not find a general bound of ∥x∥ anywhere. Here we prove that the bound on ∥x∥ is true in general. Before that, we first introduce a lemma from [1].

Lemma1

Let a=(a1​,…,an​) be a vector of positive integers with at least 2 elements, it does not contain 1 and gcd(a)=1. If gk​=gcd(ak​,…,an​), then there exist a solution to i=1∑n​xi​ai​=1 such that for all 1≤i≤n−1, ∣xi​∣≤2gi​gi+1​​ and ∣xn​∣≤2max(a1​,…,an−1​)​.

Theorem2

Let a be a vector of positive integers such that gcd(a)=1, then there exists a integral solution to x⋅a=1 such that ∥x∥≤21​(min(a)+max(a)).

Proof

Let a=(a1​,…,an​). Let an​=min(a). We can assume 1 is not in a, otherwise we can find x such that ∥x∥=1. Let gi​=gcd(ai​,…,an​). Hence gn​=min(a). We consider a solution to ∑i=1n​xi​ai​=1 satisfies Lemma 1. Let I={i∣gi+1​≥2gi​,i≤n−1} and j=min(I). One can algebraically check that a/b≤a−b holds if both a≥2b and b≥2. In particular, we have gi​gi+1​​≤gi+1​−gi​ for all i∈I∖{j}. i∈I∑​∣xi​∣≤21​i∈I∑​gi​gi+1​​≤2gj+1​​+21​i∈I,i=j∑​gi+1​−gi​≤2gj+1​​+21​i=j+1∑n−1​gi+1​−gi​=21​gn​=2min(a)​.

∥x∥=i=1∑n​∣xi​∣=∣xn​∣+i∈I∑​∣xi​∣≤2min(a)+max(a)​

Corollary3

Let a be a vector of integers such that gcd(a)=1, then there exists a integral solution to x⋅a=1 such that ∥x∥≤∥a∥.
