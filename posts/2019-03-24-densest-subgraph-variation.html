Densest subgraph variation


Let G=(V,E) be a graph. Consider an edge weight function w:E→R+ and a vertex cost function c:V→R+.

We are interested in finding S⊂V, such that w(E(S))−c(S) is maximized.

This is very close to the densest subgraph problem, as it is basically the Lagrangian relaxation of the problem.

This problem is equivalent to a min-st-cut computation on a suitable graph. Indeed, minimizing w(E(S))−c(S) is equivalent to minimizing c(S)+21​w(E(S,Sˉ))+21​∑v∈Sˉ​deg(v). This can be solved easily by modeling it as a min-st-cut.
