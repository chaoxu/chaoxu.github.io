TSP, Max TSP and Supnick


Problem1

Given f and x1​,…,xn​. Find a permutation π that maximizes (minimizes) i=1∑n​f(xπ(i)​,xπ(i+1)​).

All our index calculations are mod n.

Assume f can be evaluated in O(1) time. The TSP problem reduces to this one.

Definition2Supnick

For all x≤x′, y≤y′, f:R2→R is called Supnick if it has the following properties:

  1. Monge: f(x,y)+f(x′,y′)≤f(x′,y)+f(x,y′).
  2. Symmetric: f(x,y)=f(y,x).

The name Supnick comes from Supnick matrix, which are symmetric Monge matrices. The following theorem implies an O(nlogn) algorithm to solve the TSP problem if the distance matrix is Supnick [1].

Theorem3Supnick’s

Let x1​≤x2​≤…≤xn​, f is Supnick, then i=1∑n​f(xπ(i)​,xπ(i+1)​)

  1. is minimized when π=(1 3 5 7 … 8 6 4 2).

  2. is maximized when π=(n 2 (n−2) 4 (n−4) … 5 (n−3) 3 (n−1) 1).

Supnick matrices appears at many places. For example, maximize the area of a radar chart and find a permutation maximize sum of adjacent distance.

It is a special case of a even larger class of problem that has the above permutation as solution: Quadratic assignment problem where the matrices are monotone antimonge and benevolent symmetric toeplitz matrix [2].
